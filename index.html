About

Neuroscience taught me how intelligence works.
Engineering taught me how to build it.

I'm a student at UIUC pursuing a B.S. in Brain and Cognitive Science with a minor in Computer Science (expected December 2026). My work sits at the crossroads of neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and software engineering — with a particular focus on systems that are robust, explainable, and built for the real world.

I'm currently at Brookhaven National Laboratory building agentic pipelines that analyze how frontier LLMs reason over fMRI data and neuroscience foundation models — MCP servers exposing 40+ analysis tools, and an LLM-as-judge system that scales evaluation beyond manual review.
